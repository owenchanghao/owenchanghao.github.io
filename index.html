Journal

Opinion: We, the Chinese, need to abandon middle-kingdom mentality and welcome talents from the globe even under Covid-19 pandemic (2021-11-29)

I was reviewing Lee Kuan Yew's One Man's View of the World today. Lee Kuan Yew, the former PM of Singapore, made some worrying but rational statements of analysis about China's future when it comes to the geopolitical competition with the United States. Truly, we the Chinese processes numerous advantages. However, one of the major disadvantages we have is that in the arena of technological innovation, China is less attractive to talents around the world, thus less innovative in the modern history compared with the United States.

It's so true. We use Mandarin instead of English, which is hard for international workers to learn; we are less opened-up compared to the United States; and we are, most significantly, not welcoming international workers at the very heart of our mentality and policy-wise. It's not helpful, even toxic, when it comes to the competition with one of the most innovative country in human history.

We need to abandon middle-kingdom mentality, and welcome international workers to come to China and respect their contributions. Projects like the Sino-Singapore Knowledge City in Guangzhou are vital parts of the paradign shift. By sticking to this kind of co-operative projects, we, China, could catch up. With stratigic mistakes made by future American government (like Donald Trump's administration, disliking immigrants), China could win.
